# Brand Strategy

## Core Identity

**Name:** Packwalk
**Domain:** packwalk.ca
**Tagline:** "Every walk saves a rescue"
**Secondary tagline:** "Join the pack."

## The Big Idea

This isn't just another dog walking app. Every transaction creates impact. While Rover and Wag extract value from communities, we return it. The 20% donation isn't a marketing gimmick—it's the business model. It makes us un-copyable by incumbents who can't restructure their economics.

## Positioning

**One-liner:** The dog walking app that donates 20% to Toronto animal rescues.

**Elevator pitch:** Packwalk is the Canadian alternative to Rover—same trusted service, transparent pricing, GPS-tracked walks—but with a model that gives back. 20% of every booking goes to local shelters. For dog owners who want convenience without guilt.

**Enemy:** Faceless American platforms that extract money from Canadian communities and give nothing back.

**Hero:** Local dog owners who believe small choices add up to real change.

## Value Propositions

### For Dog Owners
1. **Trust** — Every walker is background-checked, insured, and reviewed
2. **Visibility** — GPS tracking so you see exactly where your dog goes
3. **Impact** — 20% of profits fund local shelter animals
4. **Local** — Canadian-owned, Toronto-focused, community-first

### For Walkers
1. **Fair pay** — Competitive rates, transparent fee structure
2. **Flexibility** — Set your own schedule and service area
3. **Purpose** — Your work directly helps rescue animals
4. **Community** — Part of something bigger than gig work

## Target Audience

**Primary:** Urban Toronto dog owners, 25-45
- Busy professionals who need reliable care
- Socially conscious consumers (will pay 10-15% more for ethical options)
- Active on Instagram, trust peer recommendations
- Already using apps for everything else

**Secondary:** Dog walkers looking for flexible income
- Students, freelancers, career-changers
- Animal lovers who want meaningful work
- Seeking community, not just transactions

## Voice & Tone

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Warm and direct | Corporate or stiff |
| Confident | Boastful |
| Playful when appropriate | Childish or punny |
| Honest about impact | Overpromise |
| Celebrate community | Self-congratulate |

**Example copy:**
- Yes: "Max got 47 minutes of park time today. Here's everywhere he sniffed."
- No: "Your fur baby had a pawsitively amazing adventure!"

## Visual Identity

### Color Palette
| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Ember | #C4754A | Primary actions, brand moments |
| Sage | #6B8F71 | Secondary, success states, nature connection |
| Ink | #1A1614 | Text, headers |
| Paper | #FAF8F5 | Backgrounds, breathing room |
| Gold | #C9A962 | Premium features, ratings |

### Design Principles
1. **Warm minimalism** — Premium feel without coldness
2. **Generous whitespace** — Let content breathe
3. **Subtle motion** — Spring animations, not flashy transitions
4. **Photography over illustration** — Real dogs, real walkers, real Toronto

### Typography
- Headlines: Bold, tight tracking, editorial feel
- Body: Clean, readable, generous line height
- Numbers: Tabular for prices/stats

## Name Rationale

**Packwalk** was chosen because:
- "Pack" = dogs + community (double meaning)
- Works as verb: "I'll Packwalk it"
- packwalk.ca available
- Implies belonging, not just transaction
- Canadian-neutral, not American-sounding
- "Join the pack" as natural CTA

## Summary

This brand wins by being:
1. **Specific** — Toronto-first, not trying to be everywhere
2. **Purposeful** — 20% donation is structural, not marketing
3. **Human** — Real walkers, real dogs, real impact
4. **Premium** — Design and experience justify trust

The donation angle isn't charity. It's competitive advantage. It creates word-of-mouth, earns press coverage, builds loyalty, and makes switching to Rover feel like a downgrade.
